Colosseum, Italy

Let's Know about Colosseum, Italy

  • Wonder's Name ITALY COLOSSEUM
  • Location  Rome, the capital of Italy
  • Length:  189.5 meters (620 feet) in length and 156.5 meters (514 feet) in width.
  • Construction:  The vast structure was built in the 1st century AD and features four levels of arches, a complex system of pulleys and counterweights, and a subterranean tunnel network.

Features of Colosseum, Italy

Symbol of City

  • The Colosseum in Rome, Italy is an iconic symbol of the city
  • one of the largest amphitheaters ever built.
  • Iconic ancient amphitheater that embodies the engineering and architectural prowess of the Roman Empire.

Constructions

  • It's an elliptical structure made of stone, concrete, and tuff that's 620 by 513 feet (189 by 156 meters) and four stories tall at its highest point.
  • It is an elliptical structure made of stone, concrete, and tuff, and it stands four stories tall at its highest point.

Speciality

  • A sophisticated sewage and drainage system to ensure cleanliness.
  • A complex system of pulleys, winches, and counterweights to raise and lower scenery, animals, and gladiators
  • Ornate carvings, sculptures, and decorative elements, showcasing Roman artistry.
